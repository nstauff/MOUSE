MOUSE: Bridging Microreactor Design and Economics

Microreactor Optimization Using Simulation and Economics (MOUSE) connects early-stage reactor design choices to bottom-up cost estimates, first-of-a-kind and nth-of-a-kind costs, levelized cost of energy, and market context.

What MOUSE Does

Microreactor technoeconomic analysis for early-stage scoping.

MOUSE is designed for transparent microreactor cost estimation and comparative reactor scoping. It supports nuclear microreactor design studies where users need to understand capital costs, annualized costs, fuel costs, and levelized cost of energy before detailed engineering is available.

Bottom-up cost estimation

Estimate preconstruction, direct, indirect, training, financial, operating, maintenance, replacement, decommissioning, and fuel costs.

FOAK and NOAK comparisons

Compare first-of-a-kind and nth-of-a-kind deployment economics, including capital costs and levelized cost of energy.

Interactive reactor scoping

Use the hosted Streamlit app to explore how power, enrichment, geometry, operations, tax credits, and financing assumptions affect results.

Workflow

From reactor inputs to cost and market context.

The tool combines simplified reactor design, balance-of-plant calculations, cost-account estimation, uncertainty ranges, and market benchmarks in one workflow. It is intended for research, screening, and comparative analysis, not investment or procurement decisions.

  • Estimate overnight capital cost, total capital investment, annualized cost, and LCOE.
  • Inspect full Code of Accounts cost breakdowns and cost-driver rankings.
  • Compare estimated microreactor electricity cost against selected market benchmarks.

Reactor Concepts

Reference models for three microreactor concepts.

The repository includes reference models for liquid-metal, gas-cooled, and heat-pipe microreactor concepts. These are pre-conceptual designs intended for transparent technoeconomic assessment.
